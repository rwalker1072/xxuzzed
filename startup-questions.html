100 startup questions to answer

Vision & Problem (1 – 8)

  1. What does your company do—in one sentence?
  2. Which single, painful problem are you solving?
  3. Why is now the right time to tackle it?
  4. Who experiences the pain most acutely today?
  5. How is the problem handled without you?
  6. What secret insight do you have that others don’t?
  7. How big is the “hair-on-fire” use-case versus the nice-to-have one?
  8. Five years out, what will the world look like if you succeed?

Solution & Product (9 – 16)

  1. Show me a working demo—what’s the wow moment?
  2. Walk me through a customer’s first 60 seconds.
  3. What’s unique or proprietary about your tech or approach?
  4. How quickly can you ship new features?
  5. What have users begged you to change?
  6. What has surprised you about user behavior?
  7. Where does the product break at 10× scale?
  8. What’s on the next two product road-map milestones?

Market & Customers (17 – 24)

  1. How large is the addressable market today (TAM, SAM, SOM)?
  2. How fast is that market growing?
  3. What adjacent markets could you unlock later?
  4. Who are your beach-head customers and why?
  5. How many paying customers do you have right now?
  6. What is your customer acquisition cost (CAC)?
  7. What is your ideal customer profile (ICP)?
  8. What does churn look like and why do people leave?

Traction & Metrics (25 – 32)

  1. Where do new users come from?
  2. What is your month-over-month growth rate?
  3. What is your current revenue run-rate (ARR or GMV)?
  4. What is your activation-to-retention funnel?
  5. What is your LTV/CAC ratio?
  6. What’s the single metric that matters most right now?
  7. How do physical-world ops (if any) constrain growth?
  8. Which cohort tells the best story about product-market fit?

Business Model & Revenue (33 – 40)

  1. How do you make money—subscription, usage, marketplace take-rate?
  2. What are your unit economics at maturity?
  3. How long until break-even on a per-customer basis?
  4. Where are the gross-margin killers and how do you fix them?
  5. Could competitors price you out of the market?
  6. What pricing experiments have you run?
  7. How would a 30 % drop in prices affect you?
  8. What keeps the revenue defensible over ten years?

Competitive Landscape (41 – 48)

  1. Who are the top three direct competitors?
  2. Why hasn’t a FAANG company already won here?
  3. What substitutes do customers compare you against?
  4. What will stop a fast-follower from copying you?
  5. How do you win deals today vs. the incumbent?
  6. What is your moat at scale—technology, data, network effects?
  7. Where are you weakest compared with the competition?
  8. How will the competitive set change in three years?

Technology & IP (49 – 56)

  1. Describe your tech stack—why those choices?
  2. What core capability is truly hard to replicate?
  3. Do you own any patents or defensible data assets?
  4. How have you stress-tested for security and privacy?
  5. What are the key technology risks?
  6. How do you manage technical debt?
  7. What is your roadmap for AI/automation?
  8. Who else on the team can rewrite the core system if needed?

Team & Culture (57 – 64)

  1. Why are you the right founders for this problem?
  2. How long have the founders known one another?
  3. Which critical roles are still missing?
  4. Who will be your next hire and why?
  5. What is the team’s super-power?
  6. How do you make decisions when founders disagree?
  7. How do you attract top-tier talent against better-funded rivals?
  8. What unique values shape your culture?

Operations & Execution (65 – 72)

  1. Walk me through a typical sales cycle and timeline.
  2. What are the bottlenecks in onboarding or fulfillment?
  3. How do you measure operational excellence?
  4. Which KPIs matter for customer support?
  5. What does your supply chain (or cloud spend) look like?
  6. How do you ensure quality as volume increases?
  7. What tools/processes keep everyone accountable?
  8. How would you double output with the same headcount?

Financials & Fundraising (73 – 80)

  1. What is your burn rate and runway at current spend?
  2. How much are you raising and how long will it last?
  3. What specific milestones will this round unlock?
  4. Have you considered alternative financing (debt, revenue share)?
  5. What valuations have comparable companies raised at?
  6. Describe your cap table today.
  7. How do founder salaries scale post-raise?
  8. When will you need the next round?

Go-to-Market & Growth (81 – 88)

  1. What channels bring in the best customers today?
  2. What is the payback period for each channel?
  3. How do you leverage partnerships or platforms?
  4. What experiments are in the growth backlog?
  5. What virality or network-effect loops exist?
  6. How do you localize for new geographies?
  7. What’s your strategy for enterprise vs. SMB vs. consumer?
  8. How will marketing spend scale relative to revenue?

Risks & Challenges (89 – 94)

  1. What are the top three existential risks?
  2. Which legal or regulatory hurdles could block growth?
  3. How resilient is your business to macro swings?
  4. What single metric would warn you that something is wrong?
  5. What keeps the founders up at night?
  6. What’s the biggest mistake you’ve made so far?

Exit & Future Plans (95 – 100)

  1. What’s your long-term vision—IPO, acquisition, or cash engine?
  2. Who are the natural acquirers and why?
  3. What must be true in five years for this to be a $1B company?
  4. How will you reward early employees on exit?
  5. What would you build next if capital and talent were unlimited?
  6. If this fails, why will it fail—and what would you do differently?

Solve anything. Evolve everything.
